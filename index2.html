A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

1931-2015

Missile Man of India

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam,known as the "Missile Man of India," was an esteemed scientist, visionary leader, and the 11th President of India(2002-2007). Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, he rose from humble beginnings to become a prominent aerospace scientist at ISRO and DRDO, where he contributed to India's missile and space programs. His leadership in the Pokhran-II nuclear tests showcased India's strength globally. Renowned for his humility and love for education, he inspired millions, particularly the youth, emphasizing dreams and hard work. He passed away on July 27,2015, while delivering a lecture, leaving behind a legacy of innovation and inspiration.

Biography

  • Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu.
  • Graduated in physics and earned a degree in aerospace engineering.
  • Started his career at DRDO and later joined ISRO, contributing to satellite launch vehicles.
  • Known as the "Missile Man of India" for developing Agni and Prithvi missiles.
  • Played a key role in India's nuclear tests in 1998.
  • Served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007.
  • Advocated for technological advancement through "Technology Vision 2020."
  • Received numerous awards, including the Bharat Ratna in 1997.
  • Authored several books, including the autobiography "Wings of Fire."
  • Passed away on July 27, 2015, leaving a significant legacy as a scientist and educator.
